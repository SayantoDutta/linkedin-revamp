# About skeleton

The About section is the single biggest credibility lift on LinkedIn. The first 220 characters appear on mobile before "...see more". If those 220 chars don't earn the click, the rest is wasted.

This template gives you a hook + 3-paragraph structure that works for every role.

---

## Hard rules

1. **First line is the hook.** No "Hi I'm X" intros. No "I'm passionate about". The first sentence has to make the target_person want to read the second.
2. **First 220 chars must stand alone.** If LinkedIn truncates after that, the user must still come across as compelling.
3. **No cringe.** Run the draft through `audit/cringe-detector.md` before showing.
4. **End with a CTA.** "DMs open" / email / Calendly. The user wants to be reachable.
5. **2600 character cap.** LinkedIn limit. Target 1400–1700 for readability.

---

## Skeleton

```
[Hook — 1 sentence, ≤ 220 chars, mobile-truncation-safe]

[Paragraph 1 — what the user does right now in 3–5 lines.
Use specifics. Tools, scope, outcomes. Tie to the user's biggest_result.]

[Paragraph 2 — context or origin in 3–5 lines.
What they did before. Why they ended up where they are.
Tie to anti_position — name what they're NOT, only if it sharpens the story.]

[Paragraph 3 — what's next, in 2–4 lines.
Tie to north_star.
What they want to work on next. What they want people to know about.]

[Stack line — comma-separated tools/methods/areas, if relevant.
Skip for non-technical roles.]

[CTA — 1 line.
"If you're working on X, let's talk." or "DMs open." or contact info.]

[Location line — single line. "Based in [city]."]
```

## Hook formulas

Pick by voice_mode.

### Confident hook
- "I [verb] [outcome] for [audience]."
- "[Big claim about what you do]."
- "I'm in deep build mode on [the thing]."

Examples:
- "I build AI agents end-to-end."
- "I help YC founders close their seed round in 90 days."
- "I design B2B SaaS dashboards that users actually open."

### Bold hook
- "[Provocative observation]. That's the work I do."
- "Most people in [field] do X. I do Y."
- "I'm tired of [common bad pattern]. So I [the thing you do]."

Examples:
- "Most voice AI for India sounds like a SaaS product trying to fake it. I build the kind that doesn't."
- "Founders waste 80% of their seed round on the wrong hire. I help them not."

### PAS hook (Problem · Agitate · Solve)

A three-beat opener borrowed from direct-response copy. Name a problem the target_person feels. Twist it so the cost lands. Then position the user as the answer. Compresses a whole pitch into the first few lines, so it works hard in the mobile-truncated zone.

Structure:
- **Problem:** the pain your audience already has.
- **Agitate:** the consequence or the part that stings.
- **Solve:** what you do about it, stated plainly.

Worked example (218 chars, fits the truncation zone):
```
Most internal tools die in a backlog nobody reads. The team keeps doing the work by hand, every week, forever. I build the small automations that quietly delete that work. Usually in days, not quarters.
```

When to use: voice_mode is "Bold" or "Confident", and the target_person has a clear, nameable pain. Strong for consultants, founders, and operators who sell an outcome.

### Funny hook
- One-liner that lands the joke + the credential at the same time.
- Self-aware about a LinkedIn cliche.

Examples:
- "Recovering investment banker. Currently shipping AI agents. Mostly the second one."
- "I've been called a 'rockstar engineer' on three job sites. I respond to none of them."

### Conservative hook
- Lead with the role and the audience.
- Drop the joke. Drop the provocation.

Examples:
- "Senior product manager building payments infrastructure at Stripe."
- "Independent consultant helping early-stage healthtech founders launch in the US."

## Paragraph 1 — what the user does right now

This is the meat. Use the cv_parsed + biggest_result + Phase 5 Experience drafts.

Structure:
```
Right now I'm [doing the work].

→ [Specific area 1 with detail]
→ [Specific area 2 with detail]
→ [Specific area 3 with detail]

[One-line tie-back to the hook or to north_star.]
```

The arrow bullets work especially well for builders, designers, engineers, and consultants. For sales, marketing, or operations roles, swap arrows for plain text paragraphs.

---

## Paragraph 2 — context or origin

This paragraph is optional. Include only if it strengthens the story.

Good uses:
- The user pivoted careers and the old career adds credibility ("ex-trader who now builds voice AI for fintech. The math and the markets both matter.")
- The user worked at a high-signal place ("4 years at Stripe taught me what production reliability actually means at scale")
- The user built something out of necessity ("I started building voice agents because the human callers in our funnel were ₹1,000+ per call and gave us inconsistent quality")

Bad uses:
- "I've always been passionate about..." — cringe, skip
- Generic job history that adds nothing
- Anything the Experience section already shows

---

## Paragraph 3 — what's next

Tie to north_star. This is where the user sells the future.

Structure:
```
What's interesting to me next: [Specific direction 1], [Specific direction 2], [Specific direction 3].

[Optional: the kind of work or person they want to talk to.]
```

---

## Stack line (optional)

Only for roles where tools matter — engineers, designers, marketers, data, AI.

```
Stack I work in: [Tool 1], [Tool 2], [Tool 3], [Tool 4].
```

Or:

```
Methods I lean on: [Approach 1], [Approach 2], [Approach 3].
```

---

## CTA line

One of these. Pick by what the user actually wants.

- "If you're [doing X], let's talk. DMs open."
- "Reach me at [email]."
- "Book a call: [calendly]."
- "I'm hiring/looking for [thing]. Reach out."

---

## Location line

```
Based in [city].
```

Always include. Helps recruiters and clients filter.

## Sample full About (Confident voice, builder, ~1700 chars)

This is an anonymized, synthetic example. It exists to show the *structure and voice* — never copy its content onto a real profile. Swap in the user's own work, stack, city, and goals.

```
I build [the thing you build] end-to-end.

Right now I'm in deep build mode. I work across the full stack of what a modern [your field] product actually takes to ship:

→ [Core area one]: [the specific tools/decisions, and why they matter].
→ [Core area two]: [the messy, real part most people skip].
→ [Core area three]: [what you do here, with one concrete detail].
→ [Core area four]: [the part that feeds the rest].
→ [Core area five]: [the production/deploy reality, not just the prototype].
→ [The unsolved problem you care about]: [why it's hard, what you're trying].

The pattern across all of it: I learn by building the whole thing myself, then strip out what doesn't matter. [Drafts] become [shipped work], [shipped work] becomes [proof].

What's interesting to me next: [forward-looking goal one], [goal two], and [the bigger problem you want to work on].

If you're [the kind of person you want to reach], let's talk. DMs open.

Stack I work in: [tool, tool, tool, tool. The honest list, 8–12 items].

Based in [city].
```

This skeleton is the gold standard for the Confident builder voice. Fill every bracket with the user's real material from discovery + the persona grill. Diverge from the structure only if the user's `voice_mode` or `target_person` requires it.

## Before → After rewrites

The point of this skill is the gap between these columns. Before is what a generic AI writes on the first pass. After is what survives the cringe detector and the anti-AI formatting rules. Study the moves: cut the adjectives, name the specific work, break the even rhythm, add a number, end with a real CTA. All synthetic personas.

### Hook line

Before:
> "Results-driven backend engineer passionate about building scalable, robust solutions in fast-paced environments."

After:
> "I'm the engineer who gets paged when the database falls over. Two years deep in Go and Postgres, mostly the unglamorous reliability work."

Why it's better: the Before could describe ten thousand people. The After names a person who does a specific job. Cut "results-driven", "passionate", "robust solutions", "fast-paced". Added a concrete claim and a stack.

### Opening paragraph

Before:
> "I am a dynamic consultant with extensive experience helping organizations streamline operations and drive impactful results across a wide range of industries. I leverage a holistic approach to facilitate growth."

After:
> "I fix the lifecycle email that nobody opens. Most companies send too much and convert nothing. I rebuild the sequence around one job: get the user to the thing they signed up for. Last year that meant 40% more activated accounts for a B2B SaaS team."

Why it's better: the Before is six fluff words in a trench coat ("dynamic", "extensive experience", "streamline", "impactful", "leverage", "holistic"). The After picks one specialty, names the pain, states the result. Notice the short sentence after the long one.

### Origin / pivot line

Before:
> "Throughout my career journey, I have always been passionate about technology and continuously strive to leverage cutting-edge tools to deliver value-added solutions."

After:
> "I came to this from finance, not a CS degree. Four years pricing risk taught me to distrust any model I can't break by hand. That habit is why my code ships with the edge cases already found."

Why it's better: the Before says nothing and uses three banned phrases. The After turns the unusual background into the reason to hire this person. The pivot becomes the credential.

### CTA

Before:
> "Feel free to connect! Always open to networking and exploring synergies. Let's connect and grow together!"

After:
> "If you're scaling a payments product and your reliability story is held together with hope, DM me. That's the work I like."

Why it's better: the Before is three dead CTAs stacked ("feel free to connect", "open to networking", "synergies"). The After names exactly who should reach out and why, so the right person self-selects.

## What to never produce

- Anything that starts with "Hi, I'm" or "Welcome to my profile"
- Lists longer than 7 items — they read as padding
- The phrase "I'm a [job title]" as the hook — boring
- A CTA that says "feel free to connect" — pointless
- Any sentence in the third person ("Jane is a passionate marketer who...") — never, this is your About
- The phrase "results-driven" — banned permanently

# Before / after — anonymized case studies

Three real-shape rebuilds, fully anonymized. Names, companies, numbers, and locations are changed. The *structure* of each fix is real — it's what this skill actually does.

Use these in the README and the GitHub repo. Every detail here is synthetic. No real person's data appears.

---

## Case 1 — Junior IC engineer (recent grad)

**The starting profile.** Default LinkedIn. No banner. Headline read "Software Engineer at [Company]" — i.e. the job title LinkedIn auto-fills. About section empty. Experience entries were one line each, copied from the offer letter. Skills were whatever LinkedIn suggested plus four "Forage virtual experience" badges.

**Persona grill outcome.** Goal: get hired into a better backend role within a year. Target reader: senior engineers and EMs who screen referrals. Anti-positioning: didn't want to lead with "full-stack" — wanted to be known for backend depth.

### Headline

| | |
|---|---|
| Before | Software Engineer at Acme |
| After | Backend engineer · Go + Postgres · I make slow services fast |

### About (first 220 chars — the part that shows on mobile before "see more")

> Before: *(empty)*

> After: "I'm a backend engineer who likes the unglamorous work — the query that took 900ms now takes 80, the cron job that silently failed for a month now pages someone. Two years in, mostly Go and Postgres..."

### Skills

- Dropped: 4 Forage badges, "Microsoft Office", "Teamwork"
- Added + pinned top 5: Go, PostgreSQL, Distributed Systems, API Design, Observability

**Result shape.** Profile went from "indistinguishable from every other new grad" to "obviously a backend specialist." The fix wasn't new accomplishments — it was naming the ones already there.

## Case 2 — Solo founder (1-person startup)

**The starting profile.** Headline was the company tagline ("Building the future of X"). About was three paragraphs of vision with no proof. No Featured section. Experience listed "Founder & CEO" with a two-line company description and zero outcomes.

**Persona grill outcome.** Goal: get clients / early customers, and look fundable to angels. Target reader: operators at the kind of company that would buy, plus a few angels. Anti-positioning: wanted "serial entrepreneur" *off* the profile — only one prior venture, and it felt inflated.

### Headline

| | |
|---|---|
| Before | Building the future of restaurant logistics 🚀 |
| After | I help 200+ restaurants cut food waste 30% · Founder, [Startup] |

### About hook

> Before: "We are on a mission to revolutionize the way restaurants think about their supply chain through cutting-edge AI..."

> After: "Restaurants throw away about 10% of what they buy. We've gotten our first 200 kitchens down to 7%. Here's how it works, and what I've learned building it solo..."

### Featured (was empty)

- Tile 1: the product demo (link)
- Tile 2: a customer result write-up (Notion case study — built via `/linkedin-revamp-notion`)
- Tile 3: a short "how it works" walkthrough video (built via `/linkedin-revamp-walkthrough`)

**Result shape.** The profile stopped selling a vision and started showing a working business with numbers. The "🚀 building the future" cliché — flagged by the cringe detector — was the first thing to go.

## Case 3 — Independent consultant (5+ years freelance)

**The starting profile.** Looked busy but unfocused. Headline crammed six services with slashes. About was a wall of "passionate, results-driven, dynamic" adjectives. Experience listed every client as a separate one-line entry — 14 of them — so the page read as noise. No clear "what I'm hired for."

**Persona grill outcome.** Goal: fewer, bigger contracts. Target reader: heads of marketing at Series-B+ companies. Anti-positioning: wanted to drop the "jack of all trades" feel — lead with one specialty (lifecycle marketing), mention the rest quietly.

### Headline

| | |
|---|---|
| Before | Marketing Consultant / SEO / Content / Email / Social / Strategy / Growth |
| After | Lifecycle marketing for B2B SaaS · I turn trials into paying customers |

### About (cringe detector pass)

- Flagged and cut: "passionate", "results-driven", "dynamic", "wear many hats", "think outside the box" — 5 clichés in the first paragraph alone.
- Rewrote around one proof point: a named (anonymized) before/after on trial-to-paid conversion.

### Experience

- Collapsed 14 one-line client entries into 3 grouped entries by engagement type, each with an outcome bullet and a real number.

**Result shape.** Went from "available for anything" to "the lifecycle person." Narrowing the positioning is what made it stronger — the consultant kept all 14 clients in the detail, but stopped leading with the noise.

## What every case has in common

1. **No new accomplishments were invented.** Every rewrite used material the person already had. The skill's job is to *find and frame*, not fabricate.
2. **The cringe detector earned its keep.** Every profile had at least three blocked clichés.
3. **Narrowing beat broadening.** In all three, picking *one* clear thing to be known for made the profile stronger than listing everything.
4. **The persona grill drove everything.** The "who do you want to land on your profile" answer is what made each rewrite specific instead of generic.
